A Christmas Carol

Written by Charles Dickens in 1843, "A Christmas Carol" is a timeless novella that has captured the hearts of readers for generations. Set in Victorian London, the story follows the transformation of one Ebenezer Scrooge, a miserly and cold-hearted old man, who is visited by the ghosts of Christmas Past, Present, and Yet to Come. Through these supernatural encounters, Scrooge is confronted with the impact of his greed and indifference on those around him and is ultimately inspired to embrace the spirit of Christmas, showing kindness, generosity, and compassion. The novella is celebrated for its powerful message about redemption and the importance of human connection, making it a beloved classic during the holiday season.
